How to Report Abuse

Animal cruelty remains a heartbreaking reality in many parts of the Philippines. Every day, countless dogs and cats suffer from neglect, abuse, and abandonment. Fortunately, the country has laws and organizations dedicated to protecting animals and ensuring that violators are held accountable. Knowing how to report animal abuse is a vital step toward creating a more compassionate society. The first step in reporting animal abuse is to gather proper evidence, if it is safe to do so. This includes taking clear photos or videos of the abuse or neglect. Documentation should also include the location of the incident, the date and time it occurred, and any additional context that may help investigators. If there are witnesses, it is helpful to collect their names and contact information. These details are crucial, as they strengthen the case and help authorities take swift action.Once evidence has been secured, the next step is to determine the type of abuse being reported. Common forms include physical harm, starvation, abandonment, lack of shelter, and chaining animals in cramped or unsanitary conditions. Illegal practices such as dogfighting or the sale of dog and cat meat are also classified as animal cruelty under Philippine law.
