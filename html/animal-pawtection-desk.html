How to Report Abuse

Animal cruelty remains a heartbreaking reality in many parts of the Philippines. Every day, countless dogs and cats suffer from neglect, abuse, and abandonment. Fortunately, the country has laws and organizations dedicated to protecting animals and ensuring that violators are held accountable. Knowing how to report animal abuse is a vital step toward creating a more compassionate society. The first step in reporting animal abuse is to gather proper evidence, if it is safe to do so. This includes taking clear photos or videos of the abuse or neglect. Documentation should also include the location of the incident, the date and time it occurred, and any additional context that may help investigators. If there are witnesses, it is helpful to collect their names and contact information. These details are crucial, as they strengthen the case and help authorities take swift action.Once evidence has been secured, the next step is to determine the type of abuse being reported. Common forms include physical harm, starvation, abandonment, lack of shelter, and chaining animals in cramped or unsanitary conditions. Illegal practices such as dogfighting or the sale of dog and cat meat are also classified as animal cruelty under Philippine law.

Local Animal Welfare Laws

Republic Act 8485 – Animal Welfare Act of 1998 An Act to promote animal welfare in the Philippines, otherwise known as “The Animal Welfare Act of 1998”.

The Animal Welfare Act of 1998 is the first law in the Philippines that aimed to protect the welfare of animals by prohibiting acts of cruelty towards animals, such as maltreatment, torture, killing, and neglect. The animal welfare law also regulates the sale, transport, and handling of animals to ensure their welfare. Violators of this law may face penalties, including fines and imprisonment.

Presidential Decree No. 1602 – The Anti-Cruelty Law

The Anti-Cruelty Law is penalizes acts of cruelty to animals. It includes acts such as beating, torturing, or killing animals, as well as organizing animal fights or using animals for experimentation without proper authority. The use of poison, dangerous drugs, or chemicals to kill or capture animals is also prohibited under this law.

Republic Act 10631 – Philippine Animal Welfare Act of 2013

An Act amending certain sections of Republic Act No. 8485, otherwise known as “The Animal Welfare Act of 1998”.This animal welfare law strengthens the Animal Welfare Act of 1998 by providing stricter penalties for animal cruelty. It includes higher fines and longer imprisonment terms for offenders. The law also includes provisions on responsible pet ownership, such as the requirement for pet owners to have their pets vaccinated, registered, and properly cared for.

Republic Act 9482

This Act provides for the control, prevention of the spread and eventual eradication of human and animal rabies.

Organizations to Contact

Attention as more people recognize the importance of compassion, responsible pet ownership, and the prevention of cruelty. Thankfully, there are numerous organizations across the country dedicated to the rescue, rehabilitation, and protection of dogs and cats. These groups not only provide shelter and medical care, but they also promote public education and advocacy. For individuals who want to adopt, volunteer, report abuse, or support the cause, knowing which organizations to contact is a crucial first step.One of the most established and recognized animal welfare organizations in the country is the Philippine Animal Welfare Society (PAWS). Based in Quezon City, PAWS has long been at the forefront of promoting animal rights in the Philippines. They run an animal shelter called the PAWS Animal Rehabilitation Center (PARC), where rescued dogs and cats are treated, rehabilitated, and eventually put up for adoption. They also accept reports of animal cruelty and offer legal assistance for cases that violate the Animal Welfare Act. Their work extends to lobbying for stronger laws and educating the public about responsible pet ownership. People can contact PAWS through their website (www.paws.org.ph), email (philpaws@paws.org.ph), or social media platforms.

Another key organization is CARA Welfare Philippines (Compassion and Responsibility for Animals). Located in Manila, CARA focuses on rescuing injured and abandoned animals, especially cats. They operate a sanctuary for special-needs cats and conduct trap-neuter-return (TNR) programs to manage the stray animal population humanely. CARA is also active in pet adoption and educational campaigns. Individuals may reach them through their website (www.caraphil.org) or their official Facebook page
