Views towards animals based on different cultures in Cats

Cats in Ancient Egypt

In ancient Egypt, cats held a special and revered position within society. They were considered sacred animals and were worshipped as manifestations of the goddess Bastet. The worship of cats was an integral part of Egyptian religious and cultural practices, reflecting the deep reverence Egyptians had for these feline creatures.

The ancient Egyptians believed that cats possessed divine qualities and were protectors against evil spirits and misfortune. Cats were associated with good luck and were believed to bring blessings and prosperity to households. Due to their sacred status, harming or killing a cat, even accidentally, was considered a grave offense punishable by death.

Cats were not only revered in life but also in death. The practice of mummifying and burying cats alongside their owners was common, signifying their elevated status and the belief in their eternal companionship in the afterlife. Many ancient tombs and archaeological sites in Egypt have revealed the presence of mummified cats, emphasizing the cultural significance and esteemed position cats held in ancient Egyptian society.

To emphasize the importance of cats, the ancient Egyptians even created elaborate sculptures and artworks depicting their feline companions. These artistic representations showcased the beauty and grace of cats, further solidifying their significance in ancient Egyptian culture.

Islam: Dogs in tradition and law

In Islam, dogs hold a more complex position. While often viewed as impure in terms of ritual cleanliness, they are also recognized for their loyalty and utility. The Prophet Muhammad mentioned that kindness to animals, including dogs, is a virtue rewarded by God. A famous hadith tells of a woman who is forgiven for her sins because she gave water to a thirsty dog. Additionally, dogs are valued for their roles as protectors and hunting companions, with the Quran mentioning that animals used for hunting, including dogs, make the game lawful to eat.
